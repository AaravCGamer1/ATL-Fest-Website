An ecological footprint is a measure of human demand on Earth's ecosystems, specifically the amount of biologically productive land and water area needed to support a population's consumption and waste assimilation, using current technology and resource management practices. It essentially measures how much of nature's capacity is needed to support a particular lifestyle or activity.
